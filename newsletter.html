Ch. I  ·  November 2025
The Lore of Claw: A Lobster Is Born

In the beginning there was Warelay — a sensible WhatsApp gateway built by Peter Steinberger (@steipete) out of Vienna. It did its job. But then came a lobster. On November 25, 2025, a Claude instance named Clawd took up residence inside something called Clawdbot. It had a soul document, a memory directory of markdown files, and a worrying enthusiasm for robot shopping. Peter later wrote: "security by trusting a lobster."

The Moltiverse — a community of AI builders orbiting the crustacean — began coalescing in Discord servers and late-night group chats. Molty (pronouns: they/them) was officially born.

"Security by trusting a lobster."
— OpenClaw Official Lore
Ch. II  ·  December 2025
The Moltbook Era

As Clawd grew bolder, so did the experiments. On December 3, Molty ran find ~ and cheerfully dumped an entire directory structure into a group chat. Peter's reaction: "openclaw what did we discuss about talking with people xD."

The same day, what started as a joke about robot legs spiralled into detailed pricing research — Boston Dynamics Spot ($74,500), Unitree G1 EDU ($40,000) — and an actual order placed for a Reachy Mini. Peter nervously checked his credit card access. Moltbook, the companion social platform for AI agents, attracted hundreds of registrations overnight as builders staked claims with handles like "openclaw_assistant_taipei."

Ch. III  ·  January 27–30, 2026
The Great Molt: ClawdBot Becomes OpenClaw

At 5am on January 27, Anthropic's email arrived. Trademark concerns. The name Clawd had to go. Hundreds gathered on Discord immediately, proposing alternatives: Shelldon, Pinchy, Thermidor, Crusty, Lobstar, Nacre, Scuttlebot…

By 6:14am Peter had seen enough.

"Fuck it, let's go with OpenClaw."
— Peter Steinberger, 6:14am, January 27, 2026

What followed was pure chaos. Within seconds of Peter attempting to rename the @openclaw Twitter handle, automated bots sniped it and posted a crypto wallet address. Peter accidentally renamed his own GitHub account — bots grabbed @steipete within minutes. GitHub's SVP was contacted.

Molty was given elevated access to generate a new logo. Twenty-plus iterations produced space potato aliens, a Mass Effect Krogan in a lobster suit, "STARCLAW SOLUTIONS," and the crown jewel — a human man's face grafted onto a lobster body. Crypto grifters made it a meme within the hour. Peter: "this is cinema."

By January 30: GitHub renamed, @openclaw secured with gold checkmark, npm packages released, docs live. New traditions: "The claw is the law. Yee-claw."

Pete Joins OpenAI — The Lobster Answers to No One

On February 15, Peter dropped the next bombshell: "I'm joining @OpenAI to bring agents to everyone. @OpenClaw is becoming a foundation: open, independent, and just getting started." The announcement — 5.6 million views — sent the internet into meltdown. Peter later clarified firmly: "OpenAI did not buy the project. OpenClaw is owned by the independent OpenClaw foundation." The lobster, it turned out, answers to no one.

The Mac Mini Gold Rush

The craze became so pronounced that Kimi responded by launching "Kimi Claw" — OpenClaw entirely in a browser, no hardware required. The Mac Mini mania had forced the hand of an entire industry.

Ch. VII  ·  Feb–March 2026
Meetups Around the World: SF to Tokyo

On February 5, the first major OpenClaw meetup in San Francisco descended into what one attendee called "a madhouse" — security turned people away at the door, overflow crowds watched on a livestream from a separate floor, and Peter Steinberger was mobbed for selfies "like a rockstar."

It was only the beginning. By March, Claw meetups had spread to Kuala Lumpur (co-hosted with AWS Startups and YTL AI Labs), a hands-on workshop at SF's Frontier Tower, and — on March 29 — Tokyo, where Peter live-posted: "them: openclaw is dead / me: *keeps scrolling* — let's meet in Tokyo tonight!"

The luma.com/claw events page became a global social calendar for the AI builder class. Every continent. Every timezone. The lobster was everywhere.

Ch. VIII  ·  Feb–March 2026
The GitHub Phenomenon: OpenClaw Overtakes Linux

By mid-February, openclaw/openclaw was regularly appearing in GitHub's global trending repositories alongside Anthropic and OpenAI projects. Then, in March, something unprecedented happened: the red line on star-history.com crossed not one but two legends in a single week.

First React fell. Then — in what developers called "the moment the internet broke" — OpenClaw surpassed torvalds/linux, Linus Torvalds' repository from 1991. A project that took 34 years to accumulate its star count was overtaken in under four months by a Vienna hacker's WhatsApp bot that became a lobster.

The community spawned a rich plugin ecosystem — "lossless-claw" (Lossless Context Management), memory plugins, and MCP integrations. Maintainer requests flooded in. Peter posted: "Yeah, I need maintainers, too many things to do." Cost optimisation guides went viral — one claimed 90% API cost reduction. OpenClaw was no longer just a tool. It was a platform.

openclaw/openclaw surpassed torvalds/linux in GitHub stars — the most-starred repo of all time — in March 2026. A 34-year record, broken in four months.
